Conventions and Notations used in byxtalÂ¶

  1. A fixed global orthogonal Cartesian coordinate frame is denoted by a row of basis-vectors as:

\begin{equation} \mathcal{B}_{GO} = \left[ \hat{e}_x, \hat{e}_y, \hat{e}_z \right] \end{equation}

The subscript GO refers to the fact that the reference is global (G) and orthogonal (O).

  1. A vector is expressed as the product of a row of basis vectors and a column comprising the components of this vector along the basis vectors. For example, the vector \(\vec{a}\) in the Cartesian frame is expressed as:

\begin{equation} \label{eq:vec} \vec{a} = \left[ \hat{e}_x, \hat{e}_y, \hat{e}_z \right] \begin{bmatrix} a_x \\ a_y \\ a_z \end{bmatrix} = \mathcal{B}_{GO} \begin{bmatrix} a_x \\ a_y \\ a_z \end{bmatrix} = \mathcal{B}_{GO} a_{GO} \end{equation}

Here, $ a_{GO}$ refers to the column vector of components of \(\vec{a}\) expressed in the global orthogonal Cartesian coordinate reference frame (\(GO\)).

  1. A Bravais lattice, with Pearson symbol \(P\), is denoted as \(\mathcal{B}_{P}\) and is defined by the basis vectors spanning the primitive unit cell, i.e.,

\begin{equation} \mathcal{B}_{P}= [\vec{a} ,\, \vec{b} ,\, \vec{c}] \end{equation}

  1. If one were to use the components of the vectors in the \(GO\) reference frame, then:

\begin{equation} \mathcal{B}_{P}= \mathcal{B}_{GO} [ a_{GO}, \, b_{GO}, \, c_{GO}] = \mathcal{B}_{GO} \Lambda_{P}^{GO} \end{equation}

where \(\Lambda_{P}^{GO}\) is a \(3 \times 3\) matrix of components of the basis vectors in the \(GO\) reference frame.
